Caroline King is a filmmaker, photographer, and programmer based in New York. With a keen eye for storytelling and a deep appreciation for independent cinema, she works at the intersection of visual art and curation. Her work spans from directing and screenwriting to film programming and photography, bringing a thoughtful, layered approach to every project. She has programmed for the Atlanta Film Festival, evaluating hundreds of films with a meticulous and discerning perspective. Her photography and videography work capture striking, authentic moments, whether on set or in documentary spaces. She is currently developing a short comedy film and directing a feature documentary, balancing her creative and curatorial pursuits. Caroline studied Film and Media Studies at Middlebury College, where she graduated cum laude and wrote a feature-length screenplay as her capstone project. Her work is informed by a passion for nuanced storytelling, subtle humor, and a dedication to highlighting underrepresented voices in cinema.
